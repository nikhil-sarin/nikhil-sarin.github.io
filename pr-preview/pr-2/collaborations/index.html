    Research Collaborations

    I am an active member of several major international collaborations working on gravitational waves, electromagnetic transients, and multi-messenger astrophysics.

    LIGO Scientific Collaboration (LSC)

    Role: Full member and contributor

    The LIGO Scientific Collaboration operates the Advanced LIGO gravitational-wave detectors and analyzes their data. I contribute to:

    • Parameter estimation for compact binary coalescences
    • Multi-messenger follow-up of gravitational-wave events
    • Development of inference methods (Bilby)

    Key Contributions:

    • Co-author on major GW discovery papers
    • Validation of parameter estimation pipelines
    • Multi-messenger analysis combining GW and EM data

    LIGO Scientific Collaboration

    Vera C. Rubin Observatory - LSST

    Role: Member, Transients and Variable Stars Science Collaboration

    The Rubin Observatory’s Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) will conduct a 10-year survey of the southern sky, discovering millions of transients.

    Contributions:

    • Target-of-Opportunity observation planning
    • Kilonova detection and follow-up strategies
    • Transient classification and modeling

    Recent Work:

    • Andreoni et al. 2024 - Rubin ToO 2024: Envisioning the Vera C. Rubin Observatory LSST Target of Opportunity program

    Rubin Observatory TVS

    Zwicky Transient Facility (ZTF)

    Role: Active collaborator

    ZTF is a time-domain survey discovering thousands of transients per night. I work on:

    • Broad-lined Type Ic supernova classification
    • GRB optical counterpart searches
    • Strongly-lensed supernova candidates

    Recent Papers:

    • Srinivasaragavan et al. 2024 - Optical and Radio Analysis of Systemically Classified Broad-lined Type Ic Supernovae
    • Das et al. 2025 - Low-Luminosity Type IIP Supernovae from the ZTF Census of the Local Universe

    Zwicky Transient Facility

    Einstein Probe

    Role: Collaborator on Fast X-ray Transient follow-up

    The Einstein Probe is an X-ray satellite designed to discover fast X-ray transients. I contribute to:

    • Theoretical modeling of FXT emission mechanisms
    • Multi-wavelength follow-up coordination
    • Physical interpretation of FXT properties

    Recent Einstein Probe Papers:

    • Rastinejad et al. 2025 - EP 250108a/SN 2025kg observations
    • Srinivasaragavan et al. 2025 - EP250108a: A Broad-Line Type Ic Supernova
    • Jiang et al. 2025 - EP240801a/XRF 240801B: An X-ray Flash
    • van-dalen et al. 2024 - The Einstein Probe transient EP240414a

    Einstein Probe Mission

    OzGrav - Centre of Excellence for Gravitational Wave Discovery

    Role: Alumni member (PhD from Monash University node)

    OzGrav is Australia’s leading gravitational-wave research center. My work at OzGrav focused on:

    • Neutron star merger remnants
    • Multi-messenger astronomy with GW events
    • X-ray afterglows of GRBs

    Research Themes Across Collaborations

    Multi-Messenger Astrophysics Combining gravitational waves, X-rays, optical, and radio observations to understand compact object mergers.

    Neutron Star Physics Using electromagnetic and GW observations to constrain the nuclear equation of state.

    Transient Astronomy Discovery and characterization of explosive events: kilonovae, supernovae, GRBs, TDEs.

    Statistical Methods Bayesian inference, hierarchical modeling, and machine learning for astrophysical parameter estimation.
